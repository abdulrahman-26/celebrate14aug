🕊️ Journey to Freedom

Path to Independence

A testament to the unwavering spirit and determination of millions who dreamed of a free homeland, built on the principles of faith, unity, and discipline.

1857
The First War of Independence marked the beginning of organized resistance against British colonial rule, inspiring future generations to fight for freedom and self-determination.
1906
The All-India Muslim League was established in Dhaka, providing Muslims with a unified political platform to voice their concerns and aspirations for the subcontinent.
1930
Allama Iqbal delivered his historic presidential address in Allahabad, envisioning separate Muslim states and laying the intellectual foundation for Pakistan.
1940
The historic Lahore Resolution was passed at Minto Park, formally demanding the creation of independent states for Muslims in the northwestern and eastern zones of India.
1947
Pakistan emerged as a sovereign nation at midnight on August 14th, with Muhammad Ali Jinnah as its first Governor-General. The dream of a separate homeland became reality.
1973
The Constitution of 1973 was unanimously adopted, establishing Pakistan as a federal parliamentary republic with Islam as the state religion and democratic governance.
2025
Today, we celebrate 78 glorious years of independence, democracy, and resilience as we continue building upon the vision of our founding fathers for a prosperous Pakistan.
🏴 National Symbol

Our Flag's Meaning

Every element of Pakistan's flag carries deep significance and represents the values, hopes, and aspirations that define our beloved nation.

🌙

The Crescent

Symbolizes progress and development. The crescent moon represents the bright future of Pakistan and the nation's continuous advancement toward prosperity and enlightenment.

⭐

The Star

Represents light and knowledge. The five-pointed star signifies the enlightenment that guides the nation and the divine light that illuminates Pakistan's path to greatness.

🟢

Green Field

Represents the Muslim majority and symbolizes prosperity, peace, and happiness. Green reflects Islam's significance in Pakistan's identity and the nation's agricultural abundance.

⬜

White Stripe

Represents religious minorities and symbolizes peace, purity, and honesty. It emphasizes Pakistan's commitment to protecting all citizens regardless of their faith or background.
