Journey to Freedom

Path to Independence

A testament to the unwavering spirit and determination of millions who dreamed of a free homeland.

1857
The First War of Independence marked the beginning of organized resistance against British colonial rule, inspiring future generations to fight for freedom.
1906
The All-India Muslim League was established in Dhaka, providing Muslims with a unified political platform to voice their concerns and aspirations.
1930
Allama Iqbal delivered his historic presidential address in Allahabad, envisioning separate Muslim states and laying the intellectual foundation for Pakistan.
1940
The historic Lahore Resolution was passed at Minto Park, formally demanding the creation of independent states for Muslims in the northwestern and eastern zones of India.
1942
The Pakistan National Movement gained momentum as the Muslim League launched the "Quit India" movement, demanding immediate independence from British rule.
1946
The Muslim League achieved a landslide victory in provincial elections, demonstrating overwhelming Muslim support for the creation of Pakistan.
June 1947
Lord Mountbatten announced the Partition Plan, formally agreeing to the creation of two independent dominions - India and Pakistan.
August 14, 1947
Pakistan emerged as a sovereign nation at midnight, with Muhammad Ali Jinnah as its first Governor-General. The dream of a separate homeland for Muslims became reality.
1948
Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah's famous speech outlined the principles of religious freedom, equality, and unity that would guide the new nation.
1956
Pakistan adopted its first constitution, officially becoming the Islamic Republic of Pakistan and establishing its democratic foundations.
1971
Following political turmoil and conflict, East Pakistan gained independence as Bangladesh, while West Pakistan continued as modern-day Pakistan.
1973
The Constitution of 1973 was unanimously adopted, establishing Pakistan as a federal parliamentary republic with Islam as the state religion.
1998
Pakistan successfully conducted nuclear tests, becoming the world's seventh nuclear power and the first Islamic nation to achieve nuclear capability.
2025
Today, we celebrate 78 glorious years of independence, democracy, and resilience as we continue building upon the vision of our founding fathers for a prosperous Pakistan.
National Symbol

Our Flag's Meaning

Every element of Pakistan's flag carries deep significance and represents the values that define our nation.

🌙

The Crescent

Symbolizes progress and development. The crescent moon represents the bright future of Pakistan and the nation's continuous advancement toward prosperity.

⭐

The Star

Represents light and knowledge. The five-pointed star signifies the enlightenment that guides the nation and the divine light that illuminates Pakistan's path.

🟢

Green Field

Represents the Muslim majority and symbolizes prosperity, peace, and happiness. Green reflects Islam's significance in Pakistan's identity and the nation's agricultural abundance.

⬜

White Stripe

Represents religious minorities and symbolizes peace, purity, and honesty. It emphasizes Pakistan's commitment to protecting all citizens regardless of their faith.
